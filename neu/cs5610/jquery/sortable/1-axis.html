Sortable on an Axis

By default, sortable list items can be dragged in any direction. Consider the following list:

  • Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipisicing elit, sed do eiusmod tempor incididunt ut labore et dolore magna aliqua
  • Ut enim ad minim veniam, quis nostrud exercitation ullamco laboris nisi ut aliquip ex ea commodo consequat
  • Duis aute irure dolor in reprehenderit in voluptate velit esse cillum dolore eu fugiat nulla pariatur

But if we are sorting elements that are displayed vertically, it looks better if we restrict dragging in the Y axis only. Here's the same list, but list elements can only be dragged vertically:

  • Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipisicing elit, sed do eiusmod tempor incididunt ut labore et dolore magna aliqua
  • Ut enim ad minim veniam, quis nostrud exercitation ullamco laboris nisi ut aliquip ex ea commodo consequat
  • Duis aute irure dolor in reprehenderit in voluptate velit esse cillum dolore eu fugiat nulla pariatur

The HTML of the lists is as follows:

	<ul class="sortable">
		<li class="red">Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipisicing elit, sed do eiusmod tempor incididunt ut labore et dolore magna aliqua</li>
		<li>Ut enim ad minim veniam, quis nostrud exercitation ullamco laboris nisi ut aliquip ex ea commodo consequat</li>
		<li class="blue">Duis aute irure dolor in reprehenderit in voluptate velit esse cillum dolore eu fugiat nulla pariatur</li>
	</ul>

	<ul class="sortable y-axis">
		<li class="red">Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipisicing elit, sed do eiusmod tempor incididunt ut labore et dolore magna aliqua</li>
		<li>Ut enim ad minim veniam, quis nostrud exercitation ullamco laboris nisi ut aliquip ex ea commodo consequat</li>
		<li class="blue">Duis aute irure dolor in reprehenderit in voluptate velit esse cillum dolore eu fugiat nulla pariatur</li>
	</ul>

Using jQuery, we can grab the lists by their classes as follows:

        $(function () {
            $(".sortable").sortable();
            $(".sortable.y-axis").sortable({"axis" : "y"});
        });

We add the sortable functionality to the lists by envoking .sortable() on them. The first sortable() call takes no arguments and the list sorts in its default behavior. The second sortable() takes a JavaScript object that configures the behavior of the sort. There are many other configurations you can explore here.
